About Yggdrasil

Yggdrasil is a pilot initiative geared towards leveraging Denmark, Greenland and the Faroe Island’s potential in becoming a significant contributor to the goals of the Earth Biogenome Project (EBP) - that is public release of very high quality reference genomes from as many of our planet’s eukaryotic species as possible. 

Thanks to initial funding generously provided to the University of Copenhagen’s Globe Institute by the Carlsberg Foundation that supports two technical staff at the DNRF Center for Evolutionary Hologenomics, and leveraging on a previous infrastructure award from the NovoNordisk Foundation that supports a PacBio facility at the Department of Biology, the genome sequencing and computational assembly infrastructure is now in place to allow Danish, Faroese and Greenlandic based researchers to economically generate, analyse and publicly release chromosome-level genomes of non-model eukaryotic species.

Yggdrasil principally exists to generate reference genomes for species of interest to Danish, Faroese and Greenlandic researchers, regardless of the geographic origin of the species itself – on the condition that the samples provided are of sufficient quality, and the sample provider has the legal basis for providing it. Although ultimately we hope to expand to be able to tackle any challenge, given our relatively small size there are some constraints on what we will be able to tackle, with regards to both the size and complexity of any target species’ genome, the amount of biological material available for processing, and the chemical complexity of the tissue itself.

Given our current funding enables us to cover the labour and computational costs involved in the genome sequencing, users are only required to cover the reagent costs, which enables us to provide genomes at an economical price. In return, we mandate that all genomes are immediately released to the public domain on completion of the assembly and annotation, so as to be consistent with the aims of the Earth Biogenome Project.

To learn more or discuss possible projects, please contact us either (here) or by directly emailing the Project Coordinator, Prof Tom Gilbert (tgilbert@sund.ku.dk)
